Unit 25
Unreal Conditionals: Present, Future, and Past


25.1   Present and future unreal conditionals

To form present and future unreal conditionals:

  • Use the simple past or the past progressive in the if clause.
  • Use the modals could, might, or would in the main clause.

    Pattern (25.1) finds if clauses with simple past verbs and main clauses with modals.

(25.1)
/CND/ < (/^IP/ < (/^(BED|HVD|DOD|VBD)/ !$ /CAT/)) $ MD

For example, (25.2) is found with (25.1).

(25.2)

I wonder if it would be good if you took some medicine .

    Pattern (25.3) finds if clauses with past progressives and main clauses with modals.

(25.3)
/CND/ < (/^IP/ < (/^BED/ $ (/CAT/ < /^(VAG|BAG)/))) $ /^MD\b/

For example, (25.4) is found with (25.3).

(25.4)

25.2   Past unreal conditionals

To form past unreal conditionals:

  • Use the past perfect in the if clause.
  • Use could have, may have, might have, or would have and a past participle verb in the main clause.

    Pattern (25.5) finds if clauses with past perfects and main clauses with modals + HAVE + position for a past participle verb.

(25.5)
/CND/ < (/^IP/ < (/^HVD/ $ /CAT/)) $ /^MD\b/ $ /^HV\b/ $ /CAT/

For example, (25.6) is found with (25.5).

(25.6)

If he had come , he might have fought with you .

25.3   Wishes about the present, future, and past

Pattern (25.7) finds instances of the verb WISH with an object clause (the content of the wish).

(25.7)
/^V/ < /^wish/ $ /^CP-THT-OB1/

For example, (25.8) is found with (25.7).

(25.8)

She wished that she could talk to her mother about it .
