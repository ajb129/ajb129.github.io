Unit 14

Negative Questions and Tag Questions


14.1    Negative questions

You can ask a Yes/No question by giving any of the following words an initial sentence placement:

  • a modal word (MD: can, will, ought)
  • a finite form of HAVE (HVD: had) (HVP: has, have)
  • a finite form of BE (BED: was, were) (BEP: is, am, are)
  • a finite form of DO (DOD: did) (DOP: does, do)

A negative question is made when the negation word not (NEG) or its contracted variant n't (NEG;_clitic_) is also present in the sentence. Pattern (14.1) finds negative questions.

(14.1)
/^NEG/
  $ /\bSBJ/=sbj
  $,, (/^(MD|HVD|HVP|BED|BEP|DOD|DOP)\b/=op $.. =sbj)
  >> (/^IP-SUB/=ip > (/^CP-QUE/ [ !> (__ > __) | > /^utterance/ ]) !<< (/^IP\b/ << =op) !<< (/^W/ < __ !>> (/^IP\b/ >> =ip)))

14.2    Tag questions

To find tag questions, you can use pattern (14.7).

(14.7)
/^PRN/ > /^IP-MAT/ < (/^CP-QUE/ < (/^IP-SUB/ <-1 /^NP-SBJ/))

Pattern (14.7) returns example sentences like (14.8).

(14.8)

Things to do:

1.
Observe in the hits returned by pattern (14.7) how the subject internal to the tag question (e.g., the subject highlighted in (14.8)) is a pronoun with a form that will match the subject of the matrix clause. Is this always the case?
2.
Observe in the hits returned by pattern (14.7) how the presence or absence of negation inside the tag question can alternate with the absence or presence of negation inside the main clause. Can you find cases where such alternation doesn't occur?
3.
Do tag questions invite answers or are they used rhetorically?
