Chapter 5

Clause layers


5.1    Introduction

This chapter introduces clause (IP) layers. Section 5.2 has rules for identifying clause structure. Section 5.3 focuses on examples of matrix interrogative clauses where the subject is not under question. Section 5.4 expands the potential for clause structure with catenative verbs. Section 5.5 adds rules to join up clause content for statement word order. Section 5.6 covers ripple effects in the clause that existential there starts.


5.2    Clause structure

This section provides rules for identifying clause structure content, from initial elements through to the subject and then onwards to the start of the verb with its complements. The clause_top_layer rules of (5.1) in section 5.2.1 and (5.2) in section 5.2.2 will establish initial elements up to and including the clause subject. All have a Store parameter with internal left and right structure to list index information associated with identified clause elements that await integration into the clause and that originate from either the left or right outer edges of clause structure. Also, all have a clause Type parameter, which can be set to:

  • statement_order
  • inversion_order
  • imperative_clause
  • tag_question
  • matrix_interrogative
  • matrix_constituent_interrogative

5.2.1    Non-recursive clause top rules

The clause_top_layer rules of (5.1) are non-recursive, with each saying something about the clause subject. For [rule 2], with its clause Type setting of imperative_clause, there can be no subject. The other rules need to:

  • [rules 1 and 3–6] find subject content or subject indicating content with a call of subject (from section 2.5)
  • [rule 7] satisfy the subject condition with a stored np(ICH) index for a clause with its Type set to matrix_constituent_interrogative
(5.1)
clause_top_layer(statement_order,Store,L,L0) -⁠-⁠>
  subject(SbjType,L,L1),
  clause_middle_layer(Store,SbjType,L1,L0).
clause_top_layer(imperative_clause,Store,L,L0) -⁠-⁠>
  verb_phrase_layer(Store,filled_sbj,imperative,active,L,L0).
clause_top_layer(tag_question,store([],[]),L,L0) -⁠-⁠>
  do_operator_layer(L,L1),
  subject(filled_sbj,L1,L0).
clause_top_layer(tag_question,store([],[]),L,L0) -⁠-⁠>
  have_be_or_md_finite_layer(_,L,L1),
  subject(filled_sbj,L1,L0).
clause_top_layer(Type,Store,L,L0) -⁠-⁠>
  {
    member(Type,[matrix_interrogative,matrix_constituent_interrogative,inversion_order])
  },
  do_operator_layer(L,L2),
  subject(SbjType,L2,L1),
  verb_phrase_layer(Store,SbjType,do_supported_infinitive,active,L1,L0).
clause_top_layer(Type,Store,L,L0) -⁠-⁠>
  {
    member(Type,[matrix_interrogative,matrix_constituent_interrogative,inversion_order])
  },
  have_be_or_md_finite_layer(Code,L,L2),
  subject(SbjType,L2,L1),
  verb_complements_top_layer(Code,Store,SbjType,active,L1,L0).
clause_top_layer(matrix_constituent_interrogative,store([np(ICH)|Left],Right),[node('NP-⁠SBJ',[ICH])|L],L0) -⁠-⁠>
  {
    member(SbjType,[filled_sbj,derived_sbj])
  },
  clause_middle_layer(store(Left,Right),SbjType,L,L0).

    [rules 1 and 3–6] of (5.1) call subject. They vary in regard to where the content identified by subject occurs in the word list and the value taken by the clause Type parameter, as follows:

  • [rule 1] (with a clause Type setting of statement_order) the subject call finds its content as an initial portion of the word list
  • [rules 3 and 4] (with a clause Type setting of tag_question) the content identified with subject has final position in the word list, coming immediately after content identified with do_operator_layer [rule 3] or have_be_or_md_finite_layer [rule 4]
  • [rules 5 and 6] (with a clause Type setting of either matrix_interrogative, matrix_constituent_interrogative or inversion_order) the subject call finds its content as a portion of the word list that is immediately after content identified with do_operator_layer [rule 5] or have_be_or_md_finite_layer [rule 6] and immediately before content identified with verb_phrase_layer [rule 5] or verb_complements_top_layer [rule 6]

    [rules 2–4 and 7] of (5.1) restrict how the clause SbjType parameter is set:

  • filled_sbj for [rules 2–4]
  • filled_sbj or derived_sbj for [rule 7]

    When there is clause Infl (inflection) information in (5.1), occurring with calls to verb_phrase_layer, this varies:

  • [rule 2] imperative
  • [rule 5] do_supported_infinitive

    When there is clause Voice information in (5.1), occurring with calls to verb_phrase_layer [rules 2 and 5] or verb_complements_top_layer [rule 6], this is always active.

5.2.2    Recursive clause top rules

In addition to the non-recursive rules of (5.1), there are also the recursive clause_top_layer rules of (5.2).

    There are rules for:

  • [rule 1] a clause initial adverbial, possibly followed by non_final punctuation
  • [rule 2] an adverbial that is clause final, possibly preceded by non_final punctuation

    Changing the clause type information from matrix_interrogative into matrix_constituent_interrogative, there are rules for:

  • [rule 3] a clause initial interrogative item for a stored item
  • [rule 4] a clause initial adverb phrase with type interrogative that has a -NIM (unselected adverbial) function

    Giving a stored item for a statement_order clause, there is:

  • [rule 5] a clause initial displaced item marked as a topicalized element (-TPC); the displaced item will have type established

    Finally, there is also a rule for:

  • [rule 6] conjuncts of coordinate clause structure
(5.2)
clause_top_layer(Type,Store,L,L0) -⁠-⁠>
  {
    ( Type==statement_order -⁠>
      member(Type2,[statement_order,inversion_order])
    ;
      Type2=Type
    )
  },
  initial_adverbial(L,L2),
  optional_punc_non_final(L2,L1),
  clause_top_layer(Type2,Store,L1,L0).
clause_top_layer(Type,Store,L,L0) -⁠-⁠>
  clause_top_layer(Type,Store,L,L2),
  optional_punc_non_final(L2,L1),
  adverbial(L1,L0).
clause_top_layer(matrix_interrogative,store(Left,Right),L,L0) -⁠-⁠>
  {
    gensym('-⁠',Index),
    atom_concat('*ICH*',Index,ICH)
  },
  interrogative_item(ICH,ICH_item,Index,L,L1),
  clause_top_layer(matrix_constituent_interrogative,store([ICH_item|Left],Right),L1,L0).
clause_top_layer(matrix_interrogative,Store,L,L0) -⁠-⁠>
  adverb_phrase('-⁠NIM',interrogative,L,L1),
  clause_top_layer(matrix_constituent_interrogative,Store,L1,L0).
clause_top_layer(statement_order,store(Left,Right),L,L0) -⁠-⁠>
  {
    gensym('-⁠',Index),
    atom_concat('-⁠TPC',Index,Extra),
    atom_concat('*ICH*',Index,ICH)
  },
  displaced_item(ICH,ICH_item,Extra,L,L2),
  optional_punc_non_final(L2,L1),
  clause_top_layer(statement_order,store([ICH_item|Left],Right),L1,L0).
clause_top_layer(Type,Store,[node('ILYR',[node('ILYR',IL)|CL])|L],L) -⁠-⁠>
  clause_top_layer(Type,Store,IL,[]),
  clause_top_tail(Type,Store,CL,[]).

    [rule 6] of (5.2) identifies non-initial conjuncts with clause_top_tail of (5.3).

(5.3)
clause_top_tail(Type,Store,[node('CONJP',[CONJ,node('ILYR',IL)])|L],L) -⁠-⁠>
  conj(CONJ),
  clause_top_layer(Type,Store,IL,[]).
clause_top_tail(Type,Store,[PU,node('CONJP',[node('ILYR',IL)])|L],L0) -⁠-⁠>
  punc(non_final,[PU],[]),
  clause_top_layer(Type,Store,IL,[]),
  clause_top_tail(Type,Store,L,L0).

    Interrogative items with [rule 3] of (5.2) and topicalized items with [rule 5] of (5.2) receive an index coindexed with an *ICH* (interpret constituent here marker) that goes into the left side of the Store parameter to ultimately be:

  • matched to a required element for a verb_complements_top_layer call from chapter 4
  • taken as a subject contribution by [rule 7] of (5.1), which is only possible with an index matching a noun phrase that a [rule 3] call of (5.2) identifies

    [rule 3] of (5.2) calls interrogative_item of (5.4) to introduce an initial item carrying interrogative type information.

(5.4)
interrogative_item(ICH,np(node(ICH,[])),Ext,L,L0) -⁠-⁠>
  noun_phrase(Ext,interrogative,L,L0).
interrogative_item(ICH,pp(node(ICH,[])),Ext,L,L0) -⁠-⁠>
  preposition_phrase(Ext,interrogative,L,L0).
interrogative_item(ICH,pp_lgs(node(ICH,[])),Ext,[node(Label,[node('P-⁠ROLE;_lgs_',[node(Word,[])]),NP])|L],L) -⁠-⁠>
  {
    atom_concat('PP',Ext,Label)
  },
  [w('P-⁠ROLE;_lgs_',Word)],
  noun_phrase('',interrogative,[NP],[]).
interrogative_item(ICH,adjp(node(ICH,[])),Ext,L,L0) -⁠-⁠>
  adjective_phrase(Ext,store([],[]),interrogative,L,L0).
interrogative_item(ICH,advp(node(ICH,[])),Ext,L,L0) -⁠-⁠>
  adverb_phrase(Ext,interrogative,L,L0).

    Presence of an interrogative item coincides with a change in the setting of the clause Type parameter from matrix_interrogative to matrix_constituent_interrogative. Consequently, with a matrix clause, at most one stored index related to an item with interrogative type information is possible. Moreover, when under question, the noun phrase subject of a matrix clause must be the one indexed phrase with interrogative type information.

    [rule 1] of (5.2) has an initial_adverbial call (from section 2.10) to pick up:

  • discourse coordination words (e.g. And, But)
  • clause level interjection (e.g. aah, eh, ummmmm)
  • reaction signals (e.g. good_grief, really, yes, wow)
  • formulaic expressions (e.g. good_afternoon, you_see, thank_you)
  • adverb phrases with -NIM (unselected adverbial) function
  • preposition phrases with -NIM (unselected adverbial) function
  • the subordinate component of a subordinate conjunction

An initial adverbial allows for a declarative clause which starts with a statement_order setting for its Type parameter to change to inversion_order. This makes parsing examples like (5.5) possible.

(5.5)
| ?-⁠ tphrase_set_string([w('P-⁠ROLE','Only_after'), w('ADJ','prolonged'), w('NS','discussions'), w('DOD','','did'), w('PRO','they'), w('VB',';~Ipr','come'), w('P-⁠ROLE','to'), w('D','an'), w('N','agreement')]), parse(clause_top_layer(statement_order,store([],[]))).

(
  (PP-⁠NIM-⁠TPC (P-⁠ROLE Only_after)
    (NP
      (ADJP (ADJ prolonged))
      (NS discussions)))
  (DOD did)
  (NP-⁠SBJ (PRO they))
  (VB;~Ipr come)
  (PP-⁠CLR (P-⁠ROLE to)
    (NP (D an) (N agreement))))

5.2.3    Verb phrase layer rules

[rule 2] for clause_top_layer of (5.1) calls verb_phrase_layer of (5.6). This subsequent rule layer ensures there is a place for introducing inflection and voice information before the verb and its complements are encountered. Having the clause type value imperative_clause, [rule 2] of (5.1) gives the values of imperative for the Infl (inflection) parameter and active for the Voice parameter.

    The full range of options for the Infl parameter are:

  • finite
  • imperative
  • infinitive
  • do_supported_infinitive
  • ing_participle
  • en_participle

Options for the Voice parameter are:

  • active
  • passive

    With (5.6), in addition to passing on information for verb_complements_top_layer [rule 1], there is, provided the clause has imperative inflection, a non-recursive rule [rule 2] to support the presence of an imperative DO with operator function (so tagged DO and with no verb code) possibly followed by a clitic negation (added with optional_clitic_negation from section 2.10).

    Calls to verb_phrase_layer can also be recursive, allowing the parse to:

  • [rule 3] pick up initial adverb phrases with a call to adverb_phrase (from section 3.3)
  • [rule 4] pick up clause final adverbials with a call to adverbial (from section 6.8)
  • [rule 5] start a conjunction structure
(5.6)
verb_phrase_layer(Store,SbjType,Infl,Voice,L,L0) -⁠-⁠>
  verb(Infl,Code,L,L1),
  verb_complements_top_layer(Code,Store,SbjType,Voice,L1,L0).
verb_phrase_layer(Store,SbjType,Infl,Voice,[node('DO',[node(Word,[])])|L],L0) -⁠-⁠>
  {
    SbjType == filled_sbj,
    Infl == imperative,
    Voice == active
  },
  [w('DO','',Word)],
  optional_clitic_negation(L,L1),
  verb_phrase_layer(Store,SbjType,infinitive,Voice,L1,L0).
verb_phrase_layer(Store,SbjType,Infl,Voice,L,L0) -⁠-⁠>
  adverb_phrase('-⁠NIM',non_privileged,L,L1),
  verb_phrase_layer(Store,SbjType,Infl,Voice,L1,L0).
verb_phrase_layer(Store,SbjType,Infl,Voice,L,L0) -⁠-⁠>
  verb_phrase_layer(Store,SbjType,Infl,Voice,L,L1),
  adverbial(L1,L0).
verb_phrase_layer(Store,SbjType,Infl,Voice,[node('ILYR',[node('ILYR',IL)|CL])|L],L) -⁠-⁠>
  verb_phrase_layer(Store,SbjType,Infl,Voice,IL,[]),
  verb_phrase_tail(Store,SbjType,Infl,Voice,CL,[]).

    [rule 5] of (5.6) identifies non-initial conjuncts with verb_phrase_tail of (5.7).

(5.7)
verb_phrase_tail(Store,SbjType,Infl,Voice,[node('CONJP',[CONJ,node('ILYR',IL)])|L],L) -⁠-⁠>
  conj(CONJ),
  verb_phrase_layer(Store,SbjType,Infl,Voice,IL,[]).
verb_phrase_tail(Store,SbjType,Infl,Voice,[PU,node('CONJP',[node('ILYR',IL)])|L],L0) -⁠-⁠>
  punc(non_final,[PU],[]),
  verb_phrase_layer(Store,SbjType,Infl,Voice,IL,[]),
  verb_phrase_tail(Store,SbjType,Infl,Voice,L,L0).

5.2.4    An example with full clause structure

Query (5.8) illustrates how a word list can have content for the top layer of a resulting clause, placed under a clause_top_layer node for presentation as a tree structure.

(5.8)
| ?-⁠ tphrase_set_string([w('FRM','Please'), w('VB',';~I','smile')]), parse(clause_top_layer(imperative_clause,store([],[]))).

( (FRM Please) (VB;~I smile))

yes

Calls follow (5.9) for the output of (5.8).

(5.9)
clause_top_layer imperative_clause
initial_adverbial
[w('FRM','Please')]
optional_punc_non_final
[]
clause_top_layer imperative_clause
verb_phrase_layer filled_sbj imperative active
verb imperative ;~I
[w('VB',';~I','smile')]
verb_complements_top_layer ;~I filled_sbj active
verb_complements_by_code ;~I filled_sbj active
[]

    Notably, once (5.9) reaches verb_phrase_layer, the clause information has parameter settings:

  • filled_sbj for SbjType
  • imperative for Infl
  • active for Voice

With these settings, verb_complements_by_code succeeds with the empty word list as the complement information for the intransitive verb (verb with code ;~I).


5.3    Matrix interrogatives

This section focuses on examples of matrix interrogative clauses where the subject is not under question. Prior to its non-interrogative subject, such a clause can have:

  • a finite DO
  • a finite HAVE
  • a finite BE
  • a modal verb

Any of these may be followed by the contracted form of negation (n't). We need rules to match these pre-subject components, so that a parse can integrate them with calls of clause_top_layer from section 5.2 above.

5.3.1    DO operator layer

Having the do_operator_layer rule of (5.10) leads to inclusion of a finite DO and also allows for an optional presence for negation as clitic n't (integrated with optional_clitic_negation from section 2.10).

(5.10)
do_operator_layer([node(Tag,[node(Word,[])])|L],L0) -⁠-⁠>
  [w(Tag,'',Word)],
  {
    member(Tag,['DOP','DOD'])
  },
  optional_clitic_negation(L,L0).

    As example content for a constituent question that needs a do_operator_layer call to parse a finite DO, consider (5.11).

(5.11)
| ?-⁠ tphrase_set_string([w('WADV','Why'), w('DOD','','did'), w('NEG;_clitic_','n<apos>t'), w('PRO','you'), w('VB',';~I','go')]), parse(clause_top_layer(matrix_interrogative,store([],[]))).

(
  (ADVP-⁠NIM (WADV Why))
  (DOD did)
  (NEG;_clitic_ n<apos>t)
  (NP-⁠SBJ (PRO you))
  (VB;~I go))

Having the three have_be_or_md_finite_layer rules of (5.13) allows for the inclusion of:

  • [rule 1] a finite HAVE word
  • [rule 2] a finite BE word
  • [rule 3] a modal verb

5.4    Clause structure with catenative verbs

So far, we have seen clause structure with only a single verb, but a clause might include a sequence of verbs. The main verb is either the only verb of a clause or the last verb to occur in a sequence of verbs of the same clause. Verbs of a verb sequence that are prior to the main verb are called catenative verbs.

    Section 4.10 provides verb_complements_by_code rules for the full range of catenative verbs. Now we focus on resulting clause structure from catenative verbs with code:

  • ;~cat_Vi selecting a bare infinitive clause complement (section 5.4.1)
  • ;~cat_Vt selecting a to-infinitive clause complement (section 5.4.2)
  • ;~cat_Ve selecting an active past participle (-ed/-en) clause complement (section 5.4.3)
  • ;~cat_Vg selecting an active present participle (-ing) clause complement (section 5.4.4)
  • ;~cat_Ve_passive_ selecting a passive past participle (-ed/-en) clause complement (section 5.4.5)

5.4.1    Catenative verbs with bare infinitive complements

The verb_complements_by_code rule of section 4.10 for code ;~cat_Vi selects a bare infinitive clause complement with the verb_phrase_layer rules of (5.6) above.

    As an example with selection of a bare infinitive clause as the complement of a modal verb, consider (5.18).

(5.18)
| ?-⁠ tphrase_set_string([w('MD',';~cat_Vi','Can'), w('PRO','I'), w('VB',';~I','help')]), parse(clause_top_layer(matrix_interrogative,store([],[]))).

( (MD;~cat_Vi Can)
  (NP-⁠SBJ (PRO I))
  (IP-⁠INF-⁠CAT (VB;~I help)))

yes

5.5    Statement word order with clause middle layers

So far, we have only seen subjects from a subject call creating the subject of a matrix interrogative. Statement word order, which also has to include a subject, happens with clause_top_layer [rule 1] of (5.1) that first calls subject and then goes on to call clause_middle_layer of (5.31). Similarly, there are [rules 9 and 10] of (5.1) for parsing matrix interrogatives where the subject is an inherited interrogative noun phrase, which also go on to call clause_middle_layer of (5.31).

    The non-recursive rules of (5.31) set the clause voice parameter to active. They will also:

  • [rule 1] call verb_phrase_layer after adding finite clause information
  • [rule 2] integrate do_operator_layer so that this will occur after the subject and before a subsequent call of verb_phrase_layer with do_supported_infinitive clause information
  • [rule 3] integrate have_be_or_md_finite_layer so that this will occur after the subject and before a call of verb_complements_top_layer

    There are also recursive rules allowing initial adverb phrases with -NIM (unselected adverbial) function [rule 4] and coordination at this middle clause level [rule 5] with non-initial conjuncts identified with clause_middle_tail of (5.32).

(5.31)

5.6    Existential there

An existential there (tagged EX) starts the ripple effect of needing a locally present verb with an existential verb code by setting the clause SbjType parameter to there_sbj. The existential verb licensed by this SbjType setting might be the main verb (with code ;~ex_V, ;~ex_Vp or ;~ex_Vpr) or a catenative verb (with code ;~ex_cat_Vt, ;~ex_cat_Vg or ;~ex_cat_Ve_passive_). Either way, this verb itself gives rise to the additional need for an existential subject (NP-ESBJ) as one of its selected complements.

    As an example with there and an existential verb with code ;~ex_V, consider (5.55).
