Exploring Layers and Ripples:

Studying English with Computer Parsing



Alastair Butler

Hirosaki University






This book introduces computer parsing with a descriptive grammar of contemporary English.

    Once a word occurs, ripple effects start that connect following words to form an utterance. Ripple effects place constraints on what words might be and how they can be used. We can model ripple effects by supposing that words appear under layers that build up a connected tree structure for all the word content of an utterance. Parsing language is the activity of identifying layers of structure while obeying the consequences of ripple effects.

    The book provides Definite Clause Grammar (DCG) definitions that are executable code for the XSB Tabling Prolog system. These definitions create an automatic parsing system with a wide coverage of English. The system gives feedback about the acceptability and also use of the English that we try to parse.
