Group 8, Puzzle 1Private Joseph Byers was the first Kitchener volunteer to be executed.
Group 8, Puzzle 2He was 17 and under age when he enlisted in the 1st Royal Scots Fusiliers in November 1914, and was sent to France with two weeks training.
Group 8, Puzzle 3By January 1915, his inexperience and the horrors he witnessed caused him to go absent without leave with another private, Andrew Evans.
Group 8, Puzzle 4Byers pleaded guilty, believing that his candour would save him from the death sentence.
Group 8, Puzzle 5Despite being under age, he was given no representation at his trial, and he and Evans faced a firing squad at Locre on February 6.
Group 8, Puzzle 6According to rumours, one of them did not die until the third volley, leading to speculation that the firing squad had fired wide to avoid killing the youth.
